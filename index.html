General tips and tricks

  • Answer questions WHILE you're listening
  • Learn common English names
  • Watch out for common traps
  • Words or phrases that indicate possible upcoming contradiction
    • at first, originally
    • but, however, although, in fact
    • in the past, last year
    • intend, plan
  • Phrases like "both agree", "both think" in the question
    • All options will be mentioned
    • Listen for agreement (right, yes, agree, etc.)

APT Method

Before you listen

  • Skill 1: Analyze the questions before you listen
    • Read directions
    • Analyze question type
    • Take mental note of the speakers and the topic
    • Look at the organization of information
  • Skill 2: Predict answers
    • Minimum
      • Determine parts of speech (noun, verb, adjective, adverb)
      • Numbers (money, date, time)
      • Names, proper nouns
      • What traps can there be?
  • Underline keywords in the questions
    • "Landmarks" - words that can help you keep track
    • What you are listening for
    • Words that help differentiate between answers
  • Make guesses (part 1 and 4)
    • Try to guess an answer that fits both grammatically and semantically

While you listen

  • Skill 3: Track the questions as you listen
  • Answers come in order
  • Use analysis and prediction to follow along
  • Focus on the current question and the next question
  • Skip questions that you missed
  • Move on to the next question as soon as you hear an answer
  • Listen closely as sometimes speakers change their minds or correct themselves
